About Me

Hello! I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Universidad Politécnica de Madrid (UPM), specializing in Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs), numerical methods, and surrogate modeling for fluid mechanics. My research focuses on developing hybrid, data-driven approaches that combine physical principles with machine learning to efficiently and accurately solve complex partial differential equations. I am particularly interested in investigating the capabilities and limitations of PINNs in realistic engineering contexts, including flow simulations and data-driven strategies (DD-PINNs). My work also explores the interplay between numerical solvers, learning dynamics, and physical consistency in scientific computing. Through my research, I aim to contribute to the advancement of physics-based artificial intelligence as a reliable, interpretable, and scalable tool bridging computational mechanics and machine learning.

PINNs Research

What are PINNs?

Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) are neural networks that incorporate physical knowledge through governing equations, enabling efficient solution of initial and boundary value problems.

My Research Focus

My research focuses on developing novel PINN architectures for solving complex physical systems and improving training efficiency.
